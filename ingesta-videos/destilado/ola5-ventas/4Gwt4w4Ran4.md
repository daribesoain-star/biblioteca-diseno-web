## Qué enseña
Este video enseña el método "Miyagi" para manejar objeciones en llamadas en frío, categorizando las objeciones en tres tipos (dismissivas, situacionales y de soluciones existentes) y proporcionando respuestas verbatim, marcos de trabajo y técnicas de desarme emocional para convertir objeciones en conversaciones genuinas.

## Lecciones accionables
- **Método Miyagi (3 pasos):** 1) Reír y estar de acuerdo con la objeción ("Totally get it, Jason. Honestly nowadays it's hard enough to keep a tool let alone buy something new"). 2) Incentivar a que el prospecto profundice usando frases como "Just so no one calls you from my team again, would you mind giving me a sense – is it because budget is completely out for this cycle, or is it that anytime you try to buy anything you've got to go through 7,000 business cases?" 3) Vender la "prueba de manejo": "Honestly Jason, you're probably not going to buy this thing now. But often times the folks who end up getting budget are the ones who at least know what's one, two and three on their list. Would you be completely against taking a look – not for now – just so you know it's out there in case budget happens to open up?"
- **Para objeciones dismissivas (ej: "not interested"):** Usar "agree hard and get the real objection". Respuesta verbatim: "Jason, you know what? I should have figured that you would have had something in place. Hey, just so nobody from my team bugs you again – is it that you already know what we do and this just isn't a priority right now, or is it that I just called you at the worst possible time and you're not a huge fan of cold calls?"
- **Para "send me an email":** Decir "Totally, I'd be happy to do that. Let me actually put something together for you later today. Can I ask you one question just to make sure what I send over isn't totally and utterly irrelevant?" Luego hacer una mini-pitch del problema. Si hay resistencia, usar: "How about this – I'll send you the email, let's put five minutes on the calendar same time tomorrow. You can totally decline the event after you look at the email if you don't like it, and if you do like it we keep the time."
- **Para "not my responsibility":** Responder: "Totally, this one's totally on me Jason. Just so I make sure I don't call the wrong person again – I know you run People Ops, would it be in Jane's court who runs HR over there?" Si confirman que es otra persona, pedir permiso: "Would you be completely against me mentioning that we spoke? Just so she knows I'm not coming completely out of the blue."
- **Para "no budget":** Usar: "Totally man, look it's hard enough to keep a tool let alone add something new. Just so no one calls you again – would you mind giving me a sense – is it because budget for this cycle is out, or is it that any spend whatsoever requires you to do a triple backflip?" Luego: "Knowing that you already spent all of your budget Jason, I'm gonna be honest – I don't know if you're going to be able to buy this thing. But every once in a while, in the off chance that budget does open up in the org, it's the folks who know what's one, two and three on their list that end up getting it. Would you be open to taking a look – not for now – just so you know it's out there in case budget opens up in the future?"
- **Para "we do it in house" / soluciones existentes:** Usar una "trap question" que exponga una debilidad del competidor. Ejemplo: "Totally Jason, that's on me – I should have figured you all had a way to handle your client billing. Hey, just so I make sure there's really nothing here – I'm curious, how do you make sure that when your attorneys are doing their time entries, they're in line with your client's billing guidelines?" Preparar una tabla con "competitor on one side, top three things they can't do that we do on the other side" y usar la #1 como trap question.
- **Para gatekeepers:** Usar el "triple bypass approach": 1) "Hey, could you get me over to Armand? It's Nick." (sin apellido ni empresa). 2) Si preguntan "what's this regarding", dar contexto no producto: "I actually work with a couple other SC partners in the LA office – would you mind letting him know it's Nick Seeli?" 3) Si insisten, dar prueba social: "Yeah, we actually work with a couple other partners in the office helping them with their tax planning amongst other things – it's Northwestern Mutual. I actually sent Armand a note the other day – would you mind letting him know it's Nick?" Siempre terminar con una pregunta.
- **Cuando cuelgan:** Anotar "the specific words that they said right before they hung up, the date and the time". Una semana después llamar y decir: "Armand, you and I last spoke on July 17th around 1:45 in the afternoon, and at the time you were like 'hey I can't talk right now, I'm crazy busy preparing for a big meeting' and you admittedly hung up on me. Look, I know I called you out of the blue then, but I promise I did some prep for this call and this isn't just like a random spam dial. Can I at least get half a minute to share why I called you back, and then look – you can totally hang up on me from there if it doesn't make sense?"

## Reglas para agentes
- Usa "laugh and agree" como respuesta pavloviana automática ante cualquier objeción – nunca respondas con lógica a una reacción emocional.
- Cuando te digan "not interested", nunca intentes superar la objeción lógicamente – primero desarma emocionalmente con "I should have figured you had something in place".
- Nunca digas "sorry" – usa "acknowledgement" en su lugar: "I should have figured..." en vez de "I'm sorry for interrupting".
- Para objeciones situacionales (no budget, no time), nunca argumentes ROI en la llamada – quita la presión de la venta y vende solo la "prueba de manejo".
- Para objeciones de soluciones existentes, nunca ataques al competidor directamente – usa una "trap question" que haga que el prospecto descubra el gap por sí mismo.
- Con gatekeepers, nunca mientas, nunca intentes ser su amigo, nunca les hagas el pitch completo – dosifica información solo cuando te pregunten.
- Cuando un prospecto cuelga, nunca llames de inmediato fingiendo "disconnected" – espera una semana y usa las palabras exactas que dijo como ancla de guilt.
- Para "send me an email", nunca aceptes sin antes hacer una pregunta de descubrimiento – usa "can I ask you one question just to make sure what I send over isn't totally irrelevant".
- Cuando te digan "not my responsibility" pero sabes que sí lo es, sugiere llamar a otro colega – el prospecto corregirá y dirá "no, it's actually me".
- Termina cada interacción con gatekeeper con una pregunta que los obligue a actuar, no que les dé espacio para interrogarte.

## Errores comunes que evita o menciona
- **Error: Tratar las objeciones dismissivas como si fueran sobre el producto.** La realidad: "Those early dismissive objections have nothing to do with what we are selling and everything to do with they're an objection to our interruption."
- **Error: Responder con lógica a una reacción emocional.** "If you respond to 'is this a cold call' with 'well I'm not sure yet, I did some research' – they're not listening to that and you sound like every other seller."
- **Error: Ser apologético en vez de hacer acknowledgement.** "There's a big difference between acknowledgement and apologies – your prospects need acknowledgement, not apologies."
- **Error: Intentar superar la objeción de "no budget" con argumentos de ROI en la llamada.** "A lot of sellers are gonna be like 'well we produce this much ROI' – they're not listening, you're not going to convince them of that on a cold call. Get the meeting, get them in the car."
- **Error: Persistir con prospectos que tienen soluciones enquistadas (ej: "mi cuñada es el proveedor").** "Sometimes sales people get taught that persistence always wins – following up once a week until the sister-in-law dies is probably a poor use of your time."
- **Error: Con gatekeepers – mentir, intentar ser amigos, o hacer el pitch completo.** "Mistake #1: lying to the gatekeeper. Mistake #2: trying to befriend the gatekeeper. Mistake #3: trying to pitch the gatekeeper."
- **Error: Llamar de inmediato después de que cuelguen fingiendo desconexión.** "A lot of salespeople think the right thing to do is to immediately call back and be like 'hey I think we got disconnected' – that doesn't work."
