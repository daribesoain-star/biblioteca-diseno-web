How to Design an Ecommerce Website: Step-by-Step

Designing an ecommerce website follows a repeatable sequence of decisions, from picking a platform to polishing checkout. These steps build on the design elements and tips covered below, laid out in the order most businesses tackle them.

Select an ecommerce platform. Choose a platform that offers customizable templates and flexibility, so the site can be tailored to specific business needs rather than forced into a generic layout.
Define the brand's visual identity. Settle on a color palette, pick no more than two fonts (one for headers, one for body text), and decide on a photography style before building out pages.
Add trust-building elements. Publish contact information, a clear return policy, secure payment options, and customer reviews so first-time visitors feel confident buying from an unfamiliar brand.
Design product and lifestyle photography. Shoot products on plain backgrounds from multiple angles, then add lifestyle images that show products in use to reinforce the brand aesthetic.
Apply visual hierarchy and psychological design. Use color and layout strategically to draw attention to key elements like calls-to-action, guiding the customer's eye through the page.
Build for responsive, mobile-friendly display. Test the site personally across desktop, smartphone, and tablet to confirm the transactional flow works smoothly on every screen size.
Structure clear site navigation. Limit the top-level menu to a few direct labels, such as Shop, About Us, Bestsellers, and Contact Us, and move secondary links like Return Policy and Terms of Service to the footer.
Simplify the overall layout. Remove distractions like excessive banner ads, pop-ups, or competing colors that pull attention away from products and the path to purchase.
Streamline the checkout process. Add accelerated payment options such as Shop Pay, Amazon Pay, PayPal, and Stripe so shoppers can complete a purchase quickly and securely.

The best ecommerce websites have one thing in common: they center the user experience from first click to post-checkout. There are four components every ecommerce business should consider when designing a website.

1. Building trust with customers

When someone visits your store or website for the first time, they may not know anything about your brand, the quality of your products, or your commitment to making customers happy. Deals might earn their consideration, but they'll need to trust you before they make a purchase.

Customer trust is hardest to earn when you don't have any customers, so you'll want to incorporate the following trust indicators as you build your website.

Contact information

Include an email and, if possible, a phone number and a mailing address on a contact page and in the footer of your site. This type of information, along with an about page, helps potential customers feel they're buying from a real person.

A return policy

A return policy not only makes it easier for people to send back products that don't satisfy them, it actually increases sales by lowering shopping cart abandonment and instilling the customer with a sense of confidence and trust that they can send back an item if they need to, without any transaction fees.

For example, Supergoop has a comprehensive returns page and self-directed returns portal. It also boasts its free returns policy on every product page.

Supergoop
Payment security

By adding Shopify Payments to your ecommerce website, you'll give customers access to popular payment methods like Shop Pay, Apple Pay, Google Pay, and major credit cards—all with PCI-compliant security technology to protect customer information.

Add graphics or badges to show your security compliance and all the payment methods you accept.

Customer reviews and testimonials

Many ecommerce solutions let you import reviews from across the web into your site. This includes customer photos, social media messages, and even reviews posted to other retailers. These are signals to potential customers that your brand is trustworthy.

2. Creating a visually compelling experience

Perception is everything when selling your products online. People form their first impression of your ecommerce store in mere milliseconds. Making that impression count rests on high-quality images, consistent branding design, smart use of psychological design, and a clear visual hierarchy.

Here are some best practices for each of these elements:

Photography: Product photography is like an ambassador, sharing the promise of your product online. Shoot product photos on a plain background and in many angles. Lifestyle photos are important to show your product in use and in context within an environment. They also reinforce a brand aesthetic.
Color: Color is one of the most powerful tools you can use to spark interest and emotion on your ecommerce site. It can also be used to draw attention to specific sections of your website and lead the customer journey. Smart use of color can help with accessibility, too.
Fonts: While the words you write on your ecommerce site share information, it's the typography that communicates the emotion behind that information. It's a good idea to stick to two fonts and create a hierarchy between them. Choose one font as your header or title font and another to be your body font.
Psychological design and visual hierarchy: Using psychological design principles allows you to influence customers' decisions and guide their eyes with strategically placed elements, colors, and text.

Outdoor Voices' homepage features lifestyle images to communicate its brand aesthetic and an aspirational lifestyle. It focuses on limited fonts for a clean look and a clear visual hierarchy.

Outdoor Voices
3. Focusing on responsive design across devices

The average American spends 5 hours and 16 minutes on a mobile phone each day—a 14% increase from 2024. Luckily, if you're building your business with Shopify, your store is already responsive. This means the site is user friendly on different devices and screen sizes, whether customers view it on a desktop, smartphone, or tablet.

When choosing an ecommerce website design based on mobile optimization, it's best to personally test it to see if you're happy with the transactional flow. If you're not, there's a good chance your customers won't be either.

4. Designing clear site navigation

Your website navigation should help customers find your products quickly and easily. Good navigation also helps improve SEO on your site so you get found in search results.

Stick to only a few menu headers in your top-level navigation. Be clear and direct when labeling these headers—this is no time to be clever. Key headers to include in your top-level navigation could include:

Shop
About Us
Bestsellers
Contact Us

Additional navigation links can go in your footer—the section at the bottom of your website. Return Policy, Terms of Service, and Contact Us pages can be linked here.

8 best ecommerce website design tips

While you'll want to design your online store based on your established brand guidelines, there are a few best practices to consider to improve usability and customer experience. Your ecommerce site is the résumé for your online business. Whether you're a startup or building your fifth ecommerce store, keep these website design tips in mind:

1. Keep it simple

Simple is always key to making more sales in your ecommerce business. The more distractions you put on your ecommerce website (think banner ads, pop-ups, too many colors), the more it takes away from making the sale. A good ecommerce platform will have a number of ecommerce website templates to choose from to help you keep your design streamlined.

2. Express your brand

It doesn't matter if you're a photographer, beautician, or dropshipper running an online business. People want to buy from established brands they trust and identify with. Take the time to define your brand and figure out what makes you different from competitors—then apply it to your web design.

3. Use the right platform

Use the best ecommerce platform for your online store. When using a platform like Shopify, you can set up a beautiful store with powerful ecommerce features and sell online quickly. A great ecommerce platform should be flexible and easy to use, allowing you to customize your ecommerce website to your exact needs. You can use Shopify's website builder, which offers AI functionality to create your own store from scratch.

4. Have empathy

Put yourself in your potential customers' shoes. How easy is it to navigate your ecommerce website? How does it make someone feel when shopping? How easy is the checkout process? Think about what your customer needs to make a purchase in your store and design it accordingly.

5. Upload high quality images

When used appropriately, high-quality product images significantly improve conversion rates. Research shows that products with multiple high-quality photos, 360-degree views, or 3D/AR content can see conversion rates higher than those with basic images.

Invest in good product photos and use images from free stock photo websites to help increase the visual appeal throughout the shopping experience.

6. Lead with professionalism

Want to start an online store that builds trust with shoppers? You need a professional website. Customers won't give you their hard-earned money or sensitive information like credit card numbers if it's anything less.

7. Leverage social proof

When designing your ecommerce site, look for places to show social proof like positive reviews and user-generated content. The more shoppers see the good experiences other people have had with your products, the more likely they'll be to buy.

8. Focus on checkout

Even if you have a beautiful, innovative ecommerce website design, if checkout is a pain, customers may not buy from you. Make checkout for your ecommerce business simple, straightforward, and fast. Use payment gateways like Shop Pay, Amazon Pay, PayPal, Stripe, and other accelerated payment options to make it as easy as possible for shoppers to buy.

Ecommerce Website Design Examples FAQ
How do I find ecommerce website design examples for my industry?

Look for design examples within your specific product category and business size, since a jewelry brand's needs differ from a coffee roaster's or apparel store's. Browse platform theme galleries, competitor sites, and design showcase blogs, then bookmark the pages whose photography style, color palette, and navigation feel closest to the customer experience you want to create. Note recurring patterns across the sites you like, such as prominent product photography or a streamlined checkout flow, and use them as a starting point rather than a rigid template.

Can I copy design elements from other ecommerce websites?

You can borrow layout ideas, color combinations, and content structure for inspiration, but copying another brand's exact photography, logo, or written copy can create legal issues and weakens the unique identity customers use to recognize a brand. Instead, study why an element works, such as a clear call-to-action button or a trust-building returns page, then adapt that principle using your own photography, voice, and branding so the design still feels authentically yours.

How often should I redesign my ecommerce website?

There's no fixed timeline, but a design refresh is worth considering whenever conversion rates decline, your product catalog changes significantly, or your branding no longer matches your visuals. Between full redesigns, audit page speed, update product photography, and test the checkout flow with real customers to catch smaller usability issues early. Treating design as an ongoing process, rather than a one-time project, keeps the shopping experience aligned with what customers expect as trends shift.

Do these design examples work for small or newer businesses?

Yes, many of the same design principles, including simple layouts, high-quality photography, clear navigation, and visible trust signals, scale down easily for small or newer stores without requiring a big budget. Affordable templates and drag-and-drop site builders let smaller brands apply the same visual hierarchy and checkout best practices seen in larger stores, then add custom branding elements like color and typography as the business grows.

What platform were these ecommerce website design examples built on?

Most of the ecommerce sites featured as design examples are built on Shopify, using customizable themes from the Shopify Theme Store as a starting point. Store owners then adjust colors, fonts, product layouts, and navigation to match their brand before adding custom code for more advanced design elements. This approach lets a wide range of businesses, from surf shops to skincare brands, achieve a distinct, on-brand look without starting from scratch.
